Tower Blocks Corpus — Semantic Networks

Interactive visualisations of the language and document structure within the Tower Blocks archive. These plots reveal how concepts relate to each other and which documents across different archive folders share common ground.

1. Concept Network

This plot maps the vocabulary of the archive. Each dot is a word. Words are connected by a line if they regularly appear near each other in the documents — for example, “Taylor” and “Woodrow” almost always appear together, so they are tightly linked.

The colours group words into communities — clusters of words that form a conceptual unit. For instance, you will see a cluster of structural engineering terms (wall, panel, joints, concrete), another of people involved in the inquiry (Griffiths, Goodfellow, Eveleigh), and another of tenant advocacy language (residents, association, campaign).

What to look for: Words that sit between two clusters act as conceptual bridges — they connect different themes in the archive. The larger a word appears, the more frequently it is used across the corpus.

How to interact: Click and drag to rotate the plot. Scroll to zoom. Hover over a word to see how often it appears. Click a community name in the legend to show or hide it.

2. Document Network

This plot maps the documents in the archive. Each dot is a single document (a letter, report, transcript, drawing, etc.). Documents are connected by a line if they use similar language — the more vocabulary they share, the closer they appear.

Orange lines are particularly important: they connect documents from different archive folders. These cross-archive links reveal where the same people, buildings, events, or issues appear in separate parts of the collection. Grey lines connect documents within the same folder.

What to look for: Documents at the edges of a cluster, connected by orange lines to another cluster, are the key bridging documents — they link different parts of the archive together. Isolated dots with no connections contain unique material not found elsewhere in the collection.

How to interact: Hover over a dot to see the document ID, which archive folder it belongs to, its topic, and a preview of its content. Click and drag to rotate; scroll to zoom.
